Hi everyone! Ever since I was younger, I wanted to quantify choices with probability and evaluate with complete knowledge of my choices. Here, I took initiative to apply my fixation through a case study on the 2003 Columbia Accident. The event culminated from a series of decisions that was influenced through organizational leadership, which I had prior knowledge on. Organizational Leadership & Risk Analysis Review: Through the models and Fault Tree Map address the technical aspects of failure, the root cause enabling these series of mistakes lay in management. This section evaluates the behavioral and structural dynamics within the NASA Mission Management Team (MMT) that permitted the normalization of deviance. A critical leadership failure identified in the Columbia Accident Investigation Board (CAIB) report was the shift in the "burden of proof." In a healthy safety culture, operations are assumed unsafe until proven safe. Under the STS-107 MMT leadership, this paradigm was inverted: engineers felt a pressure to prove definitively that the foam strike would cause a catastrophic failure, rather than management requiring proof that the orbiter was safe to fly.
